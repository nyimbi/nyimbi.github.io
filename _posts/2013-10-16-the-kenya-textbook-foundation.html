Concept, Principles and Operation

Background
The Kenyan Government sponsored laptop program is intended to address the following issues:
a). Make IT literacy a key component of basic education
b). Create a 21st century workforce
c). Equilibriate access to information across all social strata
d). Provide a consistent top quality education to all Kenyans students

The emphasis to this point has been on hardware acquisition, with minimal thought currently given to software and content. 

Concept:
The Kenyan Textbook Foundation (KTF) is a not for profit body to be created to develop high quality freely available open source appropriate online textbooks for all levels of education in Kenya to augment the Laptop program.

Principles of operation
a). Online interactive creation of textbooks along the lines of wikibooks
b). Wide participation in content creation by teachers, learners, parents and the educational authorities
c). Provide a free alternative to purchased textbooks for schools and students
d). Comply with KIE and MoE guidelines on content form
e). Democratic but moderated content development, each contributor will be heard and considered, but the editor has the final say.
f). All content will be hyperlinked to provide deeper insight n advanced material
g). The teacher as the purveyor of instruction will be specially considered int the development of content

Vignette the first: (How does this work in real life)
The KTF will setup a wiki and publicize it on all media (newspapers, radio, TV etc). Anybody can go online and request textbooks on any subject.

Somebody can prepare an outline of the textbook in a negotiated/mediated manner online - specifying the subject matter of each part/chapter/section of the text book.

Wrinkle: People can put a bounty on any section or chapter. They can offer to pay a small sum for a person to write a section/chapter. If the content is adjudged adequate by a poll the author is paid the sum. (this is where you can come in - give the KTF a grant to fund bounties


And the rest is just the standard open source.

Other considerations:
a). The content will be available both online an offline
- Schools can get thumb drives of the content
- Students/Teachers can go online and download/read online

b). The content will be constantly updated and rated by both students and teachers

c). Engage with teacher training colleges, departments of education in universities to assign content development as assignments to their students
