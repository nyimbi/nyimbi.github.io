--- layout: post title: Words taste great! date: '2009-04-30T10:22:00.000+01:00' author: nyimbi tags: modified_time: '2009-04-30T11:25:39.684+01:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-5550057272911021788.post-8970049513639237204 blogger_orig_url: http://nyimbi.blogspot.com/2009/04/words-taste-great.html --- This is my first foray into the wonderful world of blogging and I have been thinking about how to go about it. I have, up to now, written little notes to myself which I called ruminations and always intended to investigate further. Things being as they are, I haven't made much progress in the research programme that I set for myself a decade ago and many of those ruminations lie is sad little heaps in little known directories on myriad laptops, all over the world. So I have determined to experiment with blogging, I have spent a total of 8 minutes looking up and thinking through the political, social, economic and scientific impact of blogging and it violates every tenet of privacy, conservatism and individuality that I can think of: so I am going to do it!

When breaking the rules, I was told when very young, do it with outward confidence. I am setting myself a few very simple rules, to wit:
  1. Words taste great: As I think, so I write. I make bold assertions with the sure and certain knowledge that I may be wrong and I have no qualms about eating my words, doing a volte-face, and chomping vigorously on my previous verbiage (ruminating).
  2. Eclectic: All of it goes here (when I remember to update this blog!) and not in little notes all over the place
  3. No going back: Once written, forever posted! By keeping track of where I was wrong I am better able to learn how to be wrong better. So I will not be removing posts once posted under any circumstances. (makes me more of a public nuisance and gives the egg on the face a substantially better spread)
It is utterly unlikely that this blog is going to get much of a readership, outside of all the usual suspects, so I think it is a low risk, low visibility expose of ignorance, conclusion jumping, bias, illogicality and mental arrogance.
